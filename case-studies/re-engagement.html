Re-engagement Campaign Strategy

A strategic approach to re-engaging inactive subscribers and improving list health

Strategy Overview

This framework outlines a comprehensive re-engagement strategy for inactive subscribers. The approach includes segmentation by inactivity duration, win-back campaigns with exclusive offers, preference center integration, and list hygiene processes. These tactics help improve deliverability and recover lost revenue opportunities.

Common Re-engagement Challenges

  • Large portion of subscriber base inactive for 90+ days
  • Declining sender reputation affecting deliverability
  • Wasted email sends to unengaged subscribers
  • No strategy for re-engaging inactive users
  • Risk of being marked as spam by ISPs
  • Lost revenue from disengaged customers

Strategy & Implementation

1. Segmentation Strategy

Created three segments based on inactivity duration:

  • Segment 1: 90-120 days inactive (15,000 subscribers)
  • Segment 2: 121-180 days inactive (3,000 subscribers)
  • Segment 3: 180+ days inactive (2,000 subscribers)

2. Multi-Email Win-Back Sequence

Developed a 3-email sequence for each segment:

  • Email 1: "We Miss You" - Sentimental approach with brand reminder
  • Email 2: "What's New" - Showcase new products/features
  • Email 3: "Last Chance" - Final offer with exclusive discount

3. Offer Strategy

Differentiated offers by segment:

  • Segment 1: Moderate discount + free shipping
  • Segment 2: Higher discount + free shipping
  • Segment 3: Maximum discount + free shipping

4. Preference Center Integration

Included preference update options:

  • Frequency preferences (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • Content preferences (product categories, topics)
  • Easy unsubscribe option for those truly disengaged

Expected Outcomes

When implemented effectively, this strategy typically results in:

  • Significant portion of inactive subscribers re-engaged
  • Recovered revenue from previously inactive customers
  • Improved list health and deliverability
  • Reduced unsubscribe rates through preference updates
  • Better engagement rates from re-engaged subscribers
  • Higher re-engagement rates for recently inactive subscribers vs. long-term inactive

Key Learnings

  • Segmentation is crucial - different approaches work for different inactivity levels
  • Offers need to be compelling enough to overcome inertia
  • Giving subscribers control (preference updates) reduces unsubscribes
  • It's better to re-engage or remove inactive subscribers than keep sending to them
  • Re-engagement campaigns improve overall list health and deliverability
